## What Is an Emergency Water Shut Off Valve?

An emergency water shut off valve is a control point that stops the flow of water into a property. It is used during plumbing failures such as burst pipes, major leaks, appliance line breaks, or frozen pipe incidents to prevent water damage.

Shut off valves exist at both the **property level** and the **municipal service connection level**, depending on system design and local infrastructure.

---

## Common Shut Off Valve Locations in Georgetown Homes

In Georgetown, shut off valve placement varies due to housing age, terrain, and construction style. Typical locations include:

- Near the point where the water line enters the home
- In crawl spaces or basements beneath the main living area
- Inside garages, utility rooms, or mechanical closets
- Outdoors near the foundation or at a meter box by the street

Rural properties and older homes may have less standardized placement than newer developments.

## Residential vs. Municipal Shut Off Points

- **Residential shut off valves** control water flow within the property and are the first line of defense during emergencies.
- **Municipal shut off valves** are located at or near the water meter and may require utility or licensed professional access depending on local regulations.

Homeowners are generally responsible for maintaining access to the residential shut off valve.
